Congress Chapter Notes

- Why Congress Is the Way It Is
	- The framers created Congress instead of a parliament because they feared concentration of power.
	- They wanted a system that would:
		- protect state interests
		- slow decision making
		- encourage compromise
		- prevent tyranny of the majority
	- They created a bicameral legislature.
		- House of Representatives
			- based on population
		- Senate
			- two members from each state
	- Congress shares power with the president and the Supreme Court.
		- president can veto laws
		- Supreme Court can declare laws unconstitutional
	- Congress operates with tension between:
		- centralization
			- strong leadership
			- faster decisions
		- decentralization
			- more power for individual members
			- more debate
			- stronger committee influence
